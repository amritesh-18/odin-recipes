Paratha

Description:

Paratha is a traditional Indian flatbread made from whole wheat flour, rolled out thin and cooked on a griddle with ghee or oil until golden brown and crispy. It is often enjoyed with various curries, chutneys, or yogurt.

Ingredients:

  • Whole wheat flour (atta)
  • water
  • Salt
  • Ghee or oil

Steps:

  1. Prepare Dough: Mix 2 cups of whole wheat flour with water and a pinch of salt. Knead into a smooth dough, then let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Roll Out Dough: Divide dough into balls, roll each into a 6-8 inch circle using flour for dusting.
  3. Cook Paratha: Heat a skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. Place rolled dough on it, cook for about a minute until bubbles appear.
  4. Add Ghee or Oil: Flip paratha, brush cooked side with ghee or oil. Cook for another minute, pressing gently with spatula until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Serve: Remove from heat, drain on paper towel-lined plate. Repeat with remaining dough. Serve hot with curry, chutney, or yogurt.
